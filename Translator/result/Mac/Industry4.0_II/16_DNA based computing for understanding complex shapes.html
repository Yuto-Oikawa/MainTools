1. Introduction

To make artificial systems more intelligent, robust, efficient,and adaptive, systems developers have been using nature-inspiredcomputing methods. A nature-inspired computing method refersto a phenomenon (or to a set of phenomena) found in thenatural/biological systems. For example, the nature-inspired computingmethod known as artificial neural networks, which hasbeen found effective in solving complex pattern recognition andnon-linear signal processing problems, underlies the phenomenoncalled learning/recognition exhibited by the biological organisms.

The nature-inspired computing method known as evolutionarycomputing (i.e., genetic programming, genetic algorithms, etc.),which has been found effective in solving heuristic optimizationand logic programming problems, underlies the phenomenoncalled natural selection/evolution exhibited by the natural systems.

The nature-inspired computing method known as swarmintelligence, which has been found effective in solving complexoptimization/synthesis problems, underlies the phenomenoncalled self-organization exhibited by the biological organisms.

DNA Based Computing (DBC)—the focus of this study—is anemerging nature-inspired computing method. It takes inspirationsfrom the activities underlying such genetic molecules asDNA, m/tRNA, and enzyme/protein. In fact, the informationcarryingcapacity of the genetic molecules (e.g., DNA, m/tRNA, andenzyme/protein) is extraordinarily high, and the underlying informationprocessing is extremely parallel and energy-efficient. Thisremarkable feature has been brought into the attention by Adlemanin 1990s who showed how to solve a traveling salesman problemby using the DNA strands in wet-media. Since then, DBC hasbeen found effective in solving such computational problems asNP-hard, pattern recognition, scheduling, clustering, in developingsuch structures as nano-scale mechanisms, self-repairing/adaptiverobots, logic gates, futuristic computers, in generating randomnumbers, in processing natural language and image, in developingcryptographic systems, and so on (Adleman, 1994; Lipton, 1995;Sakamoto et al., 2000; Wasiewicz et al., 2001; Guo et al., 2005;Hsieh et al., 2008; Ran et al., 2009; Ullah et al., 1997; Sakakibara,2003; Xu et al., 2006; Nie and Zhong, 2012; Muhammad et al., 2006;Stojanovic et al., 2002; Murata and Stojanovic, 2008; Gerasimovaand Kolpashchikov, 2012; Murata et al., 2013; Chen and Yang, 2010;Gearheart et al., 2010; Wu et al., 2009; Bakar et al., 2008; Lin et al.,2007; Komiya et al., 2006; Yeh et al., 2006; Lee et al., 2011; Babaei,2013; Xiao et al., 2006). In most cases, the DBC is performed in wetmedia(in vitro) through the hybridization of the relatively shortstrands of genetic molecules (short DNA, m/tRNA, protein strands).

This study sheds somelights on such shape understanding ability of the proposed DBCwith the aid of two case studies. The first case study deals a (fractalgeometry generated natural) shape called fern-leaf and the othercase study deals with a (machining experiment generated) shapecalled tool-wear (i.e., a shape of the worn-zone of a cutting tool).

The perspective of the first case study is to elucidating the shapeunderstanding ability of the proposed DBC under a great deal ofstochastic variability, whereas the perspective of the other casestudy is to enhancing the effective information/knowledge transferamong the independent but supporting working spaces underlyingan Internet-aided manufacturing system.

2. The proposed DBC

2.1. General description

Fig. 1 illustrates the outline of the proposed DBC that is based onsome previous works (Ullah et al., 1997; Ullah, 2003, 2010). The ultimategoal of the proposed DBC is to obtain some useful feedbacksin solving a given problem through the formation of DNA-, mRNA-, and protein-like sequences (Alberts et al., 1994) in dry-media(i.e., in ordinary computers). The main inspiration of the proposedDBC is the Central Dogma of Molecular Biology: “once (sequential)information has passed into protein it cannot get out again,” i.e.,mappings from DNA/RNA to DNA/RNA/protein are possible but notfrom protein to DNA/RNA/protein (Crick, 1970).

5.1. Internet-aided manufacturing (IAM)

IAM has become an important issue of research and implementation.

The systems used in IAM are by nature ubiquitous systems(Putnik and Putnik, 2010; Jeong et al., 2009; Suh et al., 2008;Yoon et al., 2012; Ullah et al., 2013a) and heavily depend on thegenerative integration among some supportive but independentworking spaces (Putnik and Putnik, 2010). The generative integrationamong some working spaces comes into being not onlybecause of the presence of semantic/syntactic rationales but alsobecause of the presence of pragmatic rationale (Putnik and Putnik,2010). The pragmatic rationale building process requires a sign(called semiotic framework) that helps build a trust on the appropriatenessof the resources (information, knowledge, commands,instructions, etc.) obtained through the Internet for a given context(Putnik and Putnik, 2010). Adding this new functionality to theIAM systems (i.e., help build pragmatic rationale for the enhancementof trustworthiness/appropriateness of resources obtainedfrom other working spaces in the ubiquitous systems) is a challenge.

To tackle this challenge, the presented DBC can be of use insome circumstances. One of the possible circumstances is schematicallyillustrated in Fig. 8.

As seen from Fig. 8, there are two arbitrary working spacesdenoted as WS1 and WS2 in an IAM system. Let WS1 and WS2 be thesubjects of generative integration. For the sake of argument, let WS1be for creating knowledge. This means that the agents (humans andmachines) in WS1 investigate some phenomena, analyze the findings,extract knowledge for the findings, document the knowledge,and alike. On the other hand, let the agents in WS2 be the potentialusers of the findings offered by WS1 for making different decisions(e.g., decisions for operation, monitoring, planning, and alike). Thefirst hurdle for establishing a generative integration is to ensurethat WS2 trusts the resources offered by WS1. If there is a trust,the relevant resources offered by WS1 might be transferred to WS2for reuse. This phenomenon is hereinafter referred to as pragmaticadaptation of resource.

6. Concluding remarks

Unlike the other nature-inspired computing methods, DBC isstill in the phase of development. Further study is needed to elucidateits ability in solving computational problems in dry-media(in ordinary computers). Nevertheless, this study sheds some lightson the DBC’s ability in understanding complex shapes. Such abilitymight be helpful in integrating supportive but independent workingspaces in the ubiquitous systems (e.g., IAM systems) as describedin Section 5. It is worth mentioning that the role of the proposedDBC here is not for achieving so-called automation but for achievingeffective man-machine integration.

The inputs (DNA/mRNA) for producing the protein are createdby some user-defined rules and reading-frame. Thus, it is somehowdifficult to predict the protein and its IC unless the user discloses therules and reading-frame. This means that even though the deterministicrules (i.e., genetic code) are used to translate a DNA/mRNAto a protein, the presented DBC is not a deterministic approach assuch.

In both case studies (fern-leaf and tool-wear), the decision contentof protein is greater than that of the DNA and binary imagedata. This means that a few element input (binary image data andDNA) transforms into a many element output (protein) due to theexecution of the presented DBC. Thus, there is a possibility that anincrease in decision content might help identify some useful featuresof IC of a protein toward solving a problem. The increase indecision content is not guaranteed for all circumstances, i.e., theA.M.M.S. Ullah et al. / BioSystems 117 (2014) 40–53 53internal setting (the DNA and mRNA synthesizing rules and thereading-frames) is an important factor for ensuring the degree ofincrease in decision content, however. This issue remains open forfurther study.

In this study, the frequencies of the elements of the proteins areused as the decision-relevant information. Other operations (e.g.,Hamming distance) intended for processing sequential informationcan be used. This issue remains open for further research. Inaddition, degeneracy (or redundancy) of the genetic code (i.e., anamino acid is coded by multiple codons) might have consideredwhile determining the IC of a protein. Further study can be carriedout to see the implication of degeneracy in the proposed DBC.

IAMで使用されるシステムは本質的にユビキタスシステムであり(Putnik and Putnik, 2010; Jeong et al., 2009; Suh et al., 2008; Yoon et al., 2012; Ullah et al., 2013a)、いくつかの支持的だが独立した作業空間間の生成的な統合に大きく依存している(Putnik and Putnik, 2010)。いくつかの作業スペース間の生成的な統合は、意味的・統語的な根拠の存在だけでなく、語用論的な根拠の存在によっても生まれます(Putnik and Putnik,2010)。語用論的根拠の構築プロセスには、インターネットを通じて入手した資源（情報、知識、命令、指示など）が、ある文脈において適切であるかどうかの信頼を構築するための記号（記号論的枠組みと呼ばれる）が必要である(Putnik and Putnik,2010)。この新しい機能をIAMシステムに追加すること（つまり、ユビキタスシステムにおいて、他の作業空間から得られるリソースの信頼性／適切性を高めるための実用的な根拠を構築する手助けをすること）が課題となっています。

この課題を解決するために、今回発表したDBCはいくつかの状況で利用できます。その一つが図8の模式図です。

図8からわかるように、IAMシステムには、WS1とWS2と呼ばれる2つの任意の作業空間がある。WS1とWS2を生成的統合の対象とする。議論のために、WS1は知識を創造するためのものとする。これは、WS1のエージェント（人間や機械）が、ある現象を調査し、その結果を分析し、その結果に応じた知識を抽出し、その知識を文書化する、ということを意味する。一方、WS2のエージェントは、WS1で得られた知見を利用して、様々な意思決定(例えば、運用、監視、計画など)を行う可能性があるとします。生成的な統合を確立するための最初のハードルは、WS2がWS1から提供されたリソースを信頼することです。信頼があれば、WS1が提供した関連リソースがWS2に転送され、再利用される可能性があります。このような現象を、以下では、リソースの実用的な適応と呼ぶ。

6. 締めくくりの言葉

DBCは、他の自然界にヒントを得た計算方法とは異なり、まだ開発段階にあります。ドライメディア（通常のコンピュータ）で計算問題を解く能力を解明するには、さらなる研究が必要です。とはいえ、今回の研究では、DBCの複雑な形状を理解する能力に光が当てられました。このような能力は、セクション5で述べたように、ユビキタスシステム（IAMシステムなど）において、サポートしつつも独立した作業空間を統合する際に役立つかもしれません。なお、ここで提案するDBCの役割は、いわゆる自動化ではなく、人と機械の効果的な統合を実現することです。

タンパク質を生成するためのインプット（DNA/mRNA）は、ユーザーが定義したルールとリーディングフレームによって作られます。そのため、ユーザーがルールやリーディングフレームを開示しない限り、タンパク質やそのICを予測することは難しいのです。つまり、DNA/mRNAをタンパク質に変換するために決定論的なルール（遺伝暗号）が使われていても、今回のDBCはそのような決定論的アプローチではないということです。

いずれのケーススタディ（シダの葉と工具の摩耗）においても、タンパク質の判定内容は、DNAや二値画像データの判定内容よりも大きくなっています。これは、少数要素の入力（2値画像データとDNA）が、提示されたDBCの実行により、多数要素の出力（タンパク質）に変換されることを意味しています。したがって、決定内容の増加は、問題解決のためにタンパク質のICの有用な特徴を特定するのに役立つ可能性があります。しかし、すべての状況で決定内容の増加が保証されているわけではなく、決定内容の増加の程度を保証するためには、内部設定（DNAやmRNAの合成ルールや読み込みフレーム）が重要な要素となります。この点については、今後の課題としたい。

本研究では，決定に関連する情報として，タンパク質の要素の頻度を用いた．逐次的な情報を処理するための他の演算（例えば、ハミング距離）を使用することもできます。この点については、今後の研究課題です。また、1つのアミノ酸が複数のコドンでコードされているような遺伝暗号の縮退（冗長性）も、タンパク質のICを決定する際に考慮されるかもしれません。提案されたDBCにおける縮退性の意味については、さらに研究を進めることができます。
