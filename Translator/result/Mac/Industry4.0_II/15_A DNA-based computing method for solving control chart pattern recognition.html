1. Introduction

Nature has been a source of inspiration for many successfulcomputational metaphors (artificial neural network, evolutionarycomputing, self-organization, membrane computing, swarm intelligence,quantum computing, DNA-based computing (DBC), and soon) [1]. This study deals with a novel DBC method. Particularinterest is to use the genetic rules that make DNA useful for naturalsystems [2]. As an application of the proposed DBC method, patternrecognition problems of statistical process control are considered.

The remainder of this paper is organized as follows:Section 2 provides a brief background of DBC and associatedmethods. Section 3 describes genetic mechanisms underlying theproposed DBC method. Section 4 describes the proposed DBCmethod. Section 5 describes why and how the proposed DBCmethod can be applied to recognize normal and abnormal patternsoften observed in the control charts often used in statistical processcontrol. Section 6 shows the performance of the proposed DBCmethod when it is applied to solve the said pattern recognitionproblem and discusses the results. Section 7 concludes.

3. DNA-centered processes and rules

Three basic processes work simultaneously to make theinformation of DNA meaningful for a natural system, as follows:DNA transcription, tRNA–amino-acid compound formation, andRNA translation. These three processes collectively exhibit a set ofrules. This section briefly describes how these rules evolve from theaforementioned processes. The detailed molecular descriptions ofthese processes can be found in [2] and in the references therein.

(b) Synthesize free bases to produce mRNA of the recognized/selected segment. 5.1. Preliminaries

Nature-inspired computing (e.g., artificial neural network,genetic algorithm, swarm intelligence, emergent synthesis, etc.)has been playing a key role in developing intelligent manufacturingsystems [21,22]. One of the challenges of manufacturingsystems is to maintain the required quality of parts manufacturedby different manufacturing processes. To ensure the quality of apart, it is important to monitor and control the underlyingmanufacturing processes. To monitor and control a manufacturingprocess, a quality control tool called control chart is used [23]. ForX-Bar control chart, in particular, if the process average exhibits anormal pattern (i.e., no specific pattern) within the control limits,then the process should be continued. Otherwise, the processshould be stopped and the causes of abnormality should beidentified and eliminated before restarting the process. Therefore,correct recognition of normal and abnormal patterns observed inthe control charts is an important issue of intelligent manufacturingsystems.

Various methods have been introduced to recognize patterns incontrol charts (e.g., normal, cyclical, upward/downward shift/trend, and mixed patterns) using such mathematical procedures asartificial neural networks [24–28], fractal dimension [29], correlationcoefficient [30], statistical-parameter-based feature modeling [31–33], quantitative space modeling [34], and hybrid modeling (e.g.,artificial neural network and wavelet, wavelet and fuzzy clustering,wavelet and decision tree) [35–39].

Even though a great deal of work has been done to tackle thechallenge of recognizing patterns observed in control charts, thisproblem needs further investigations. The reasons are explainedbelow:The issue of window size: Most of the studies have usedstochastic equations to generate data x(t), t = 1,. . .,n for patternrecognition (see [29] or [31] for these equations) and used arelatively large window size, n > 30 (i.e., using 30 or more datapoints) to show the effectiveness of the methods. In reality, adecision should be taken using a relatively shorter window sizen ffi 15 (more or less 15 data points). Otherwise, it might be too lateto take a corrective measure on time.

The issue of window size with respect to period: It is very difficultto distinguish a cyclical pattern from a normal pattern (see theregression tree in [31,33]). It becomes more challenging when theperiod (l) of a cyclical pattern is greater than the half of thewindow size (l > n/2) [29]. Therefore, more investigation isneeded to see the effectiveness of a pattern recognition methodkeeping the cycle of a pattern greater than the half of the windowsize.

The issue of normal pattern: A standard set of stochasticequations are often used to generate patterns called normal,cyclical, upward/downward shift/trend, mixed, etc. [29,31,33].

There is no guarantee that an equation intended for a patternalways produces data points representing it (intended pattern).

This is particularly true when a very short window size n ffi 15 isused. For example, consider the case of generating a normalpattern. The following equation is used to generate a time seriesx(t), t = 1,. . .,n, for a normal pattern.

A set of data points x(t), t = 1,. . .,n can be mapped into proteinsusing the presented DBC. Fig. 9 illustrates the process of mapping aset of data point into proteins.

7. Conclusions

A novel DBC method is developed that consists of artificial DNAgeneration, artificial DNA transcription, and artificial RNA translation.

Artificial DNA generation produces a DNA-like sequence froma given time series. Artificial DNA transcription generates mRNA bygrafting as set of DNA-like sequences. Artificial RNA translationproduces protein-like sequence using the genetic rules found in thenatural systems among four letters of DNA and twenty letters ofproteins plus one special letter for stop codon/anti-codon.

The presented DBC method is applied to recognize patternsoften observed in control charts of statistical process control. It isshown that even though the window size (number of data points ofcontrol chart) is kept very short (less than and equal to 15), thepresented DBC method does not produce any false alarm (does notmisunderstand an abnormal pattern as a normal pattern). Furtherstudy is needed to deal with the undecided patterns.

3. DNAを中心としたプロセスとルール

DNAの情報が自然界のシステムにとって意味のあるものになるためには、DNAの転写、tRNA-アミノ酸化合物の形成、RNAの翻訳という3つの基本的なプロセスが同時に作用します。この3つのプロセスには、それぞれルールがあります。ここでは、これらのプロセスがどのように進化していくのかを簡単に説明します。なお、これらのプロセスの詳細な分子構造については、[2]およびその文献に記載されている。

7. 結論

人工DNAの生成、人工DNAの転写、人工RNAの翻訳からなる新しいDBC法を開発しました。

人工DNA生成では、与えられた時系列からDNA様の配列を生成します。人工DNA転写では、DNA様の配列を接ぎ木してmRNAを生成します。人工RNA翻訳は、DNAの4文字とタンパク質の20文字に加えて、ストップコドン／アンチコドンの1文字を加えた自然界の遺伝法則を用いて、タンパク質様の配列を生成します。

発表されたDBC法は、統計的工程管理の管理図によく見られるパターンを認識するために適用されました。その結果、ウィンドウサイズ（管理図のデータポイント数）を非常に短くしても（15以下）、発表したDBC法は誤報を出さない（異常なパターンを正常なパターンと誤認しない）ことが示された。確定していないパターンへの対応は今後の課題である。
